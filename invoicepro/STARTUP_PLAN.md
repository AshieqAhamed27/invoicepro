# InvoicePro Startup Plan

## Positioning

InvoicePro should compete as a cash-collection product, not as a generic invoice generator.

Recommended positioning:

`InvoicePro helps Indian freelancers, agencies, and consultants send invoices, share payment links, and collect recurring client payments faster.`

## Ideal Customer Profile

Primary segment:
- Freelancers and agencies in India
- Consultants, developers, designers, marketers, video editors, CA firms, and service studios
- Businesses billing repeat clients every month
- Revenue stage: roughly INR 20,000 to INR 300,000 per month

Jobs to be done:
- Create clean invoices quickly
- Share a payment link by email or WhatsApp
- Track who has paid and who is overdue
- Reuse invoice structure for retainer clients
- Keep client and payment history in one place

## Core Problem To Solve

Customers do not mainly struggle to create invoices.

They struggle to:
- Get paid on time
- Remember follow-ups
- Manage repeat billing
- Keep payment status and client data organized

The product should always answer:

`How does InvoicePro help a service business collect money faster?`

## Product Wedge

InvoicePro should win on:
- Recurring invoices for retainers
- Fast public payment links
- Simple client directory from invoice history
- UPI and Razorpay-friendly collection flow
- Clean, low-friction workflow for non-finance users

It should avoid trying to become:
- Full accounting software
- Inventory software
- A broad SMB platform for every industry

## Pricing Strategy

Current recommended plans:

Free
- INR 0
- 2 invoices
- Public payment page
- Basic client history

Pro Monthly
- INR 499 per month
- Unlimited invoices
- Payment links
- Recurring invoices

Pro Annual
- INR 4,999 per year
- Everything in Pro Monthly
- Better yearly value
- Priority support

Future Business plan:
- INR 1,499 to INR 2,999 per month
- Team access
- Approval flows
- Accountant exports
- Advanced collections reporting

## 90-Day Roadmap

### Days 1-30

Goal: make the product story clear and improve conversion.

Ship:
- Sharp homepage focused on late payments and recurring billing
- Cleaner checkout and pricing page
- Better in-app language
- Reminder actions surfaced in dashboard
- First-run onboarding for freelancers and agencies

### Days 31-60

Goal: improve retention and repeat usage.

Ship:
- Estimate or proposal flow that converts to invoice
- WhatsApp sharing shortcuts
- Better reminder workflow
- Overdue tracking view
- Branded invoice templates

### Days 61-90

Goal: become a true collections product.

Ship:
- Recurring payment setup research and integration path
- Failed payment recovery workflow
- Customer portal improvements
- Basic collection analytics
- Accountant-ready export pack

## Go-To-Market

Start with founder-led distribution.

Channels:
- Direct outreach to agencies and freelancers
- Twitter/X and LinkedIn content around invoicing pain
- WhatsApp demos
- Personal onboarding calls

Messaging:
- Not "AI invoicing"
- Not "all-in-one finance stack"
- Use "stop chasing payments every month"
- Use "turn repeat work into repeat collections"

## Metrics

Track these every week:
- Time to first invoice
- Trial to paid conversion
- Number of invoices created per active workspace
- Percent of invoices paid
- Time from invoice created to payment received
- Number of recurring invoices created

## What To Build Next

Highest-priority product opportunities:
1. Better reminder workflow
2. Proposal to invoice conversion
3. Recurring collection support
4. Payment recovery dashboard
5. Team and accountant workflows

## Notes On Competition

Competing as a generic invoicing tool is difficult because established products already offer free invoicing tiers.

InvoicePro should differentiate on:
- Simplicity
- Repeat client billing
- Faster collections
- India-friendly payment behavior
